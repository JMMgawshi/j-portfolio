Design Lead at EnergySavvy Dylan Wilbanks:

I've never met a great designer who wasn't a perfectionist.
They sweated tiny details, worried about pixel perfection,
and could cite a dozen mistakes they'd made in even the most successful design.
But what set them apart was controlling their need for perfection in order to ship their work.
Learning when to say, 'It's good enough' and telling your inner perfectionist to live with it;
that's a hard skill to master. And it's essential if you want to succeed.
